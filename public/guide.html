Getting Started

Welcome to SOVEREIGN, a real-time multiplayer geopolitical strategy game. You lead a nation, manage its economy, build a military, forge alliances, and compete on the world stage.

Creating Your Account

Register with a username, email, and password. You'll receive a 6-digit verification PIN via email (or on-screen if email isn't configured). Enter the PIN to verify your account.

Founding Your Nation

After registration, create your nation by choosing:

  • Nation Name — A unique name (3-50 characters)
  • Leader Name — Your title and name
  • Government Type — Each has unique bonuses and penalties
  • Continent — Grants a permanent resource production bonus
  • Flag — Pick a pattern and choose 3 colors
💡 New Player Protection: New nations receive 168 turns of "beige" protection — you cannot be attacked during this time, but you also cannot attack others. Use this time to build up your economy!

Economy

Your economy is the backbone of your nation. Everything — military, buildings, spies — costs money and resources. Strong management keeps you competitive.

Resources

There are 8 resources in SOVEREIGN. Each is produced by specific city improvements.

Resource Produced By Used For
💰 Money Taxes, Commerce buildings Everything — the universal currency
🌾 Food Farms (50/turn each) Population growth, infantry upkeep
🔩 Steel Iron Mines (20/turn) Tanks, ships, missiles
🛢️ Oil Oil Wells (10/turn) Vehicles, aircraft, navy
⚙️ Aluminum Bauxite Mines (10/turn) Aircraft, carriers
💣 Munitions Munitions Factory (15/turn) Infantry, artillery, missiles
☢️ Uranium Uranium Mines (3/turn) Nuclear warheads, ballistic missiles
💎 Rare Minerals Rare Mines (2/turn) Drones, nukes, missile defense

Income & Expenses

Each turn, your nation earns income from taxes (based on population × tax rate) and commerce (from banks, supermarkets, malls, stadiums). Expenses include building upkeep and military maintenance.

⚠️ Negative Treasury: If your treasury drops below zero, 5% of your military units will desert each turn until you recover.

City Improvements

Build improvements in your cities to produce resources, generate power, boost commerce, and expand your military capacity. Each improvement has a cost, upkeep, and a per-city limit.

Category Examples Purpose
⚡ Power Coal Plant, Oil Plant, Nuclear Plant, Wind Farm All improvements need power to operate
🌾 Resource Farm, Iron Mine, Oil Well, Bauxite Mine, Uranium Mine Produce raw resources each turn
🏦 Commerce Bank, Supermarket, Mall, Stadium Generate money income
🏥 Civil Hospital, Police Station, Subway, Recycling Center Boost approval, reduce crime/pollution
🏗️ Military Barracks, Arms Factory, Air Base, Drydock, Missile Pad Unlock and house military units

Infrastructure & Land

Each city has infrastructure and land. Higher infrastructure increases your tax income and population capacity. Infrastructure costs scale with current levels — the cost formula is: base × (1 + currentInfra / 500).

Market Trading

Buy and sell resources on the world market at fluctuating prices. Prices change each turn based on supply/demand. There is a 5% markup on trades.

💡 Trade Limits: Max 500 units per transaction, up to 10 trades per hour. Watch market prices to buy low and sell high!

Approval & Stability

Approval reflects how your people feel about your leadership. It's affected by tax rate, social spending, government type, wars, and civil improvements. Stability represents internal order — affected by wars, government type, police stations, and random events. Both range from 0 to 100.

🚨 War Exhaustion: Each active war costs you 2 approval and 3 stability per turn. Avoid long, multi-front wars!

War Declaration

To declare war, several conditions must be met:

  • Target must be within your war range (score × 0.4 to score × 1.6)
  • Neither side can be under beige protection
  • No active Non-Aggression Pact between you
  • Max 3 offensive wars at the same time
  • 12-turn cooldown before re-declaring war on the same nation
  • Unprovoked war costs -30 approval

Battle System

In each war, both sides get 3 action points per turn. Each battle costs 1 AP. Choose your attack type:

Attack Type Uses Terrain Effects
🪖 Ground Assault Army Weak in mountains (×0.6), forests (×0.8)
✈️ Air Strike Air Force Slightly reduced in forests, urban
🚢 Naval Bombardment Navy Strong on coast (×1.3), weak inland
🚀 Missile Strike Strategic No terrain modifiers
⚔️ Combined Arms All branches No terrain modifiers

Defense Postures

Posture Defense × Counter Chance Preserve Units

Counter Attack ×1.0 40% 0%
Guerrilla ×1.2 20% 30%
Strategic Retreat ×0.5 0% 70%

War Resolution

Wars end when one side reaches 100 war score, or when a player offers peace. The winner receives 10% of the loser's treasury and resources as loot.

Diplomacy

Build relationships with other nations through alliances and treaties. Diplomacy can be more powerful than military strength alone.

Alliances

Create or join an alliance to cooperate with other nations. Alliance features include:

  • Alliance Bank — Deposit and withdraw shared funds (founder only for withdrawals)
  • Roles — Founder, Officer, and Member with different permissions
  • Alliance Chat — Private messages visible only to alliance members
  • Bio/Description — Officers+ can edit the alliance description
  • Member Management — Promote, demote, or kick members (must outrank target)

Treaties

Abbr Treaty Effect
MDP Mutual Defense Pact Both nations agree to defend each other if attacked
ODP Optional Defense Pact Optional assistance in defense — no obligation
NAP Non-Aggression Pact Prevents either side from declaring war on the other
TRADE Trade Agreement Mutual trade relationship
EMBARGO Embargo Block trade with the target nation
💡 NAP Strategy: A Non-Aggression Pact must be cancelled before you can declare war. Use NAPs to protect your flanks while focusing on other threats.

Messaging

Communicate with other nations through the in-game messaging system to negotiate, threaten, or coordinate strategy.

Message Types

Type Description
💬 Normal Standard diplomatic communication
⚔️ War Demand Ultimatums and war-related demands
📦 Trade Offer Proposing resource trades to another nation
🔔 System Automated notifications from game events

Inbox Features

Your inbox shows the 50 most recent messages with unread indicators. You receive real-time notifications via WebSocket when a new message arrives.

⚠️ Rate Limit: You can send up to 10 messages per hour to prevent spam. Messages are sanitized — no HTML tags allowed.

Espionage

Deploy your spies on covert missions against enemy nations. Each spy is consumed per mission, and there's a cooldown per target.

Spy Missions

Mission Cost Risk Effect
🔍 Gather Intel $5,000 10% Reveals enemy military, money, score
💣 Sabotage $10,000 30% Destroys 5 infrastructure in a random city
📢 Propaganda $8,000 20% Reduces target's approval by 10
🔬 Steal Tech $15,000 40% Gain +10 Rare Minerals
🗡️ Assassinate $50,000 60% -30 Stability, -20 Approval to target

The Risk column shows the chance of your spy being caught. A caught spy is publicly announced in world news.

🚨 Cooldown: 6-turn cooldown between spy missions against the same target. Plan your espionage campaign carefully!

World & Rankings

The global stage is your playing field. Track the balance of power, read world news, and see where your nation ranks.

Nation Scoring

Your score is calculated from:

  • Infrastructure — 2 points per infrastructure level
  • Population — 1 point per 1,000 citizens
  • Military — Units × their strength rating
  • Treasury — 1 point per $1,000

Turn Ticks

The game world advances in ticks every 2 hours. Each tick processes:

  • Resource production and consumption
  • Income collection and expense deduction
  • Population growth
  • Approval and stability updates
  • Market price fluctuation
  • Active war processing
  • Random world events
